Chapter Rules

While we do abide by the rules dictated by the MGA Rulebook, we still tend to have some issues with our pace of play. As a result, we have come up with a few of our own rules that supersedes the MGA rulebook on a few topics. When playing in a Columbus MGA event, the following rules will take priority:

[ NOTE - not really a chapter rule, but be aware that we never go back to a previous spot after you've already advanced. ]

  • If you come across a scenario that you arenâ€™t sure how to handle, come to an agreement among everyone in your group on how to proceed. Then confer with Jason / Jeff after the round to see if the most appropriate action was taken


  • All penalties will cost you 1 stroke


  • An area marked as Out of Bounds is to be treated like a lateral water hazard


  • If you are unsure if your ball went OB, you can declare and hit only 1 provisional ball

    • If your original ball is still in bounds, you must play that ball
      • No Penalty

    • If your original ball is OB, you must play your provisional ball
      • 1 Stroke Penalty

    • If your original ball is OB and you did not hit a provisional ball, you must drop where your original ball went OB
      • 1 Stroke Penalty

    • If your original ball is OB -AND- your provisional is OB, you must drop where your original ball went OB
      • 1 Stroke Penalty


  • "Lift - Clean - Place" will always be in effect for all lies EXCEPT bunkers

    • This only applies when the ball has plugged in the ground or is visibly muddy

    • Using this rule only for the purpose of trying to improve your lie will result in a 1 stroke penalty
      • Cheaters suck!


  • BUNKERS - Always try to play out of them, but:

    • Standing water anywhere in the bunker

      • The bunker is considered unplayable

      • Pull the ball out of the bunker on line with the flag, but no closer to the hole
        • No Penalty

    • The condition of the bunker is like hitting off of concrete

      • Lift your ball and try raking a spot for you to drop the ball and hit
        • No Penalty

      • Move to a more playable area of the bunker and drop the ball and hit
        • No Penalty


    • If the bunker is just that AWFUL and there is a risk of injury or club damage

      • Pull the ball out of the bunker on line with the flag, no closer to the hole
        • No Penalty
